World Happiness Report (2022)

The World Happiness Report is a publication of the Sustainable Development Solutions Network, powered by the Gallup World Poll data. The World Happiness Report reflects a worldwide demand for more attention to happiness and well-being as criteria for government policy. It reviews the state of happiness in the world today and shows how the science of happiness explains personal and national variations in happiness.

Bubble Plot showing World Happiness

This bubble plot showcases multiple attributes. Position channel is used to encode quantitative attributes like social support and freedom to make life choices. Size channel is used to encode log GDP per capita and colour hue channel is used to encode region. Social support and freedom to make life choices are attributes ranging from 0 to 1. The closer the value is to 1, the higher social support and/or freedom people have. By examining the bubble plot, viewers can quickly discern patterns and relationships between these four dimensions of data. For example, they can identify regions where both social support and freedom are high (bubbles clustered in the upper-right corner of the plot).

Choropleth Map showing World Happiness by GDP Per Capita

GDP per capita is often used as a measure to determine happiness. The higher the GDP per capita, the happier people in that country tend to be, and vice versa. From the visualisation, it is evident that people living in higher income countries such as United States, Australia, and European countries report higher GDP per capita, suggesting they experience higher levels of happiness.
